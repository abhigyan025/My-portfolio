Meet Kumar Abhigyan

Kumar Abhigyan is a young Indian author, student, and analytical thinker focused on geopolitics, global economics, and youth intellectual engagement. Growing up in India, he developed a deep curiosity about global power structures, currency systems, religion, and how narratives shape political reality.

From an early age, Abhigyan gravitated toward complex global discussions that many adults hesitate to approach. Rather than limiting himself to surface-level commentary, he began studying the architecture of global systems — how the US dollar achieved dominance after Bretton Woods, how trade deficits shape monetary leverage, and how emerging powers explore alternatives to dollar-based settlements.

His analytical style blends structured reasoning with conviction. He examines not just what is happening, but why institutions behave the way they do. For instance, dollar dominance is not merely about currency preference — it is tied to military influence, financial infrastructure, SWIFT control, and trust networks. In his work, he questions whether de-dollarization efforts represent genuine systemic change or strategic diversification.

Beyond geopolitics, Abhigyan studies youth political psychology and the digital transformation of discourse. Social media platforms have altered attention spans, reward outrage over nuance, and amplify emotional polarization. He critiques these patterns while acknowledging their role in democratizing voice.

His intellectual development has been shaped by debate culture. Structured argumentation, counter-claim analysis, and evidence-based reasoning form the foundation of his thinking. He believes ideas must withstand scrutiny rather than rely on volume.

As an author of "The World From an Indian Teenager's POV," he presents reflections on society, faith, national identity, and global order — not as conclusions imposed on the reader, but as structured explorations meant to provoke independent thought.

His long-term vision is to build an intellectual brand grounded in clarity, courage, and disciplined reasoning. Rather than chasing viral commentary, he aims to contribute sustained analysis that evolves with global shifts.

The World From an Indian Teenager's POV

This book explores global politics, society, religion, identity, and the economic systems that shape modern life. It presents reflections from a young Indian perspective navigating an interconnected yet divided world.

The book examines how currency dominance affects sovereignty, how faith intersects with national development, and how emerging economies challenge established hierarchies. It questions whether de-dollarization is a symbolic shift or structural transformation.

Abhigyan explores India’s evolving role in global geopolitics — balancing strategic autonomy while navigating multipolar tensions. He analyzes how youth perception differs from traditional elite narratives.

The text combines analytical commentary with introspective reflection. It is not merely descriptive; it is interpretive. It challenges assumptions about power, morality, and global order.

By presenting structured arguments rather than emotional claims, the book positions itself within a growing space of youth intellectual literature that seeks clarity over chaos.
