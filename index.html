Meet Kumar Abhigyan

Kumar Abhigyan is a young Indian author and analytical thinker focused on global economics, currency dominance, de-dollarization, and geopolitical power transitions. Growing up in a rapidly evolving India, he represents a generation observing the transformation of global order in real time.

Read More

Born in 2010, Kumar developed early curiosity about how the world functions beyond surface-level narratives. While many teenagers consume global events passively, he studies the structure beneath headlines: how reserve currencies operate, how oil trade reinforces dollar dominance, and how emerging economies attempt to reshape financial systems.

His intellectual focus includes the mechanics of the US dollar as the global reserve currency, the strategic expansion of BRICS nations, bilateral trade agreements bypassing dollar settlements, and the implications of digital currencies in sovereign policy. He analyzes how economic leverage influences geopolitical power, and how information ecosystems amplify or distort these shifts.

As a student, he balances academics with independent research, reading about monetary policy, global trade routes, sanctions systems, and institutional power frameworks. He believes youth perspectives deserve space in serious discussions about world order, economic independence, and national growth trajectories.

His long-term vision is to build a structured intellectual platform centered on data-backed reasoning, disciplined argumentation, and analytical writing that contributes to discussions on global economic transitions.

The World From an Indian Teenager's POV

This nonfiction work reflects on identity, economy, society, and global power structures through the lens of a young Indian observer navigating modern complexity.

Read More

The book examines themes including nationalism, globalization, faith, digital influence, economic disparity, and generational responsibility. It explores how India positions itself within global systems dominated by Western financial architecture and how youth interpret these transitions.

It addresses de-dollarization trends, the psychological impact of global competition, cultural contradictions, and the role of independent thought in a hyper-connected world. Rather than offering rigid ideology, it encourages analytical thinking and intellectual courage.

Designed for young readers and policy-curious audiences, the book bridges personal reflection with macro-level observation, creating a hybrid between memoir-style thought and geopolitical commentary.

Essays

Current essays focus on the dominance of the US dollar in global trade and the growing movement toward de-dollarization led by emerging economies.

Read More

Topics include the petrodollar system, reserve currency mechanics, sovereign debt structures, sanctions leverage, and currency swap agreements between emerging markets. Additional essays analyze how social media platforms influence political opinion formation and economic narratives among youth populations.

Future research will examine digital currency frameworks, central bank digital currencies (CBDCs), strategic commodity leverage, and India's role in shaping a multipolar economic future.
