Meet Kumar Abhigyan

Kumar Abhigyan is an Indian writer exploring society, geopolitics, and global systems through structured reflection and critical inquiry.

Read More ↓

Born in October 2010 and based in Jharkhand, India, Abhigyan developed an early curiosity about global affairs, economic systems, and faith in contemporary society. His work reflects an analytical approach shaped by structured debate and sustained intellectual exploration.

He has participated in numerous online debates focused on geopolitics and global power structures, earning recognition for clarity and persuasive reasoning. His writing aims not to provoke reaction, but to encourage thoughtful engagement with complex issues.

Through essays and commentary, he seeks to understand how power, ideas, and institutions shape the world — and how a critically thinking generation interprets those shifts.

Dollar Dominance and De-Dollarization

The United States dollar remains central to global trade and reserve systems. Its dominance is supported by liquidity, institutional trust, and financial infrastructure developed over decades.

Continue Reading ↓

Discussions of de-dollarization have gained traction among emerging economies seeking financial autonomy. However, replacing a reserve currency involves more than political alignment; it requires deep capital markets, stability, and sustained global confidence.

While diversification efforts may gradually reshape aspects of trade settlement, systemic change is likely to unfold incrementally rather than abruptly.

Social Media and Modern Discourse

Social media platforms have reshaped the architecture of public opinion, amplifying voices and accelerating information flow.

Continue Reading ↓

Algorithms prioritize engagement, often rewarding emotional intensity over depth. This dynamic can foster polarization while simultaneously broadening access to global conversations.

For younger generations, digital platforms are primary arenas of political and cultural exposure, demanding critical engagement and media literacy.
