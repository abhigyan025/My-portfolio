Meet Kumar Abhigyan

Kumar Abhigyan is a young Indian author, student, and independent thinker focused on global affairs, world economy, geopolitics, and societal transformation. Born in 2010, he represents a generation growing up in a rapidly shifting global order.

His intellectual interests include de-dollarization, currency dominance, emerging multipolar power structures, India's economic future, and the influence of digital media on political narratives. From an early age, he developed a strong curiosity about how global systems operate — from central banking policies to geopolitical alliances.

Beyond writing, Kumar actively engages in debate and analytical discussions. His arguments often revolve around macroeconomic trends, the role of the US dollar in global trade, BRICS expansion, energy markets, and the reshaping of international economic order.

As a student, he balances academic responsibilities with research, writing, and continuous learning. His work reflects a belief that young voices deserve space in serious global conversations. He aims to build a platform rooted in data-driven thought, structured reasoning, and intellectual honesty.

This website serves as his official platform for essays, reflections, and long-term intellectual projects.

The World From an Indian Teenager's POV

The World From an Indian Teenager's POV is a nonfiction exploration of society, identity, religion, economy, and global transformation through the lens of a young Indian mind.

The book reflects on contradictions within modern India, cultural pride, global perception, and personal growth. It questions narratives, examines structural systems, and discusses how economic power shifts influence everyday lives.

It also addresses themes such as faith, nationalism, youth responsibility, social media influence, and the psychological impact of globalization.

Rather than presenting rigid conclusions, the book invites readers to think critically and independently. It is designed for teenagers, young adults, and anyone interested in understanding global affairs from a fresh generational perspective.
